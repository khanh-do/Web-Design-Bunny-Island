HISTORY OF BUNNY ISLAND

PAST HISTORY

In an isolated and remote area of the Inland Sea of Japan, sits the small island of Okunoshima.  Therein lies its dark past, serving as the secret location for the manufacture and testing of poisonous gas for chemical warfare during World War II.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

CURRENT DAY

Now, it’s the home of hundreds of rabbits—and they are eerily adorable!


Rumored to be the descendants of the poor test bunnies from the chemical factory (the Japanese government denies those claims) and released by officials, the rabbits are now free from predators of any kind. That’s turned them into the most curious creatures unafraid of anyone who comes to visit them.
